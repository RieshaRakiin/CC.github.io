Basic Knowledge about Typhoons and Typhoon preparedness

A typhoon is a powerful tropical cyclone that forms over the western North Pacific Ocean, specifically between 180째 and 100째 longitude in the Northern Hemisphere [1]. These storms are known for their destructive winds and heavy rainfall.

  • Region: Typhoons are distinct from hurricanes, which form in the Atlantic and Eastern North Pacific Ocean. They also differ from cyclones in other parts of the world which may have different naming conventions.
  • Wind Speed: To be classified as a typhoon, a storm must have sustained winds of at least 119 kilometers per hour (74 miles per hour) [1]. This is the same wind speed threshold for a hurricane.
  • Formation: Like other tropical cyclones, typhoons form over warm ocean waters, typically at least 26째C (79째F) at the surface [2]. The warm water provides the energy needed to fuel the storm's development. As warm, moist air rises from the ocean surface, it cools and condenses, releasing energy that powers the storm's rotation. The Coriolis effect, due to Earth's rotation, further influences the cyclone's circulation.

Before, During, After a typhoon

Before a Typhoon During a Typhoon After a Typhoon
Stay Informed - Monitor weather reports regularly and pay close attention to typhoon warnings issued by PAGASA Stay indoors - preferably on a lower floor. Stay away from windows and glass doors. Stay Alert for Dangers - Be aware of potential hazards after the storm has passed, such as downed power lines, damaged buildings, and flooding.
Prepare an Emergency Kit - Assemble a kit with food, water, first-aid supplies, radio, flashlight, cash, documents, and a multi-tool. Follow instructions - If evacuation is ordered, proceed to the designated evacuation center immediately. Check on others
Prepare your home - Secure your home by trimming branches and moving outdoor furniture. Turn of Utilities - turn off electrical and gas utilities at the main switch to prevent electrical fires or gas leaks. Report Damages - Report any damage to your property to the proper authorities.
Develop a Communication Plan - Talk to your family about typhoon plans. Have a meeting spot and communication plan.
Prepare your evacuation plan - Know your designated evacuation center and the evacuation route.
